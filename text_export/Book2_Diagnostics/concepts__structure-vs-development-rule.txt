# Structure-vs-Development Rule (Extended)

## V1 Definition (The Micro Disambiguator)

When you cannot tell whether an internal Execution problem is caused by a **skill gap** ($E_S$) or a **coordination failure** ($E_{Co}$), **fix Structure first.**

Coordination failures mask skill levels. When ownership is unclear and work collides, even skilled engineers appear to struggle because they're fighting structural friction. Clarifying structure reveals true skill levels.

## V2 Extended Definition (The Macro Disambiguator)

In Canonical V2, this rule expands significantly across the 5 Macro Blocks. When observing a symptom, it is typically ambiguous whether the root cause is team-level Execution ($E$) or systemic environment ($V, P, A$). 

**The Rule: Always assume systemic/macro friction before assuming team capability limits.**

1. **Focus vs. Incentives:** Is low Focus due to bad Execution (WIP Discipline)? Or is it driven by bad Org-Alignment (People rewarded for starting new things, not finishing)? *Assume $A$ is the friction before blaming $E$.*
2. **Quality vs. Burnout:** Is low Quality due to lacking Standards? Or is the People System exhausted ($P \approx 0$)? *Fix Sustainability/Safety before enforcing stricter rules.*
3. **Clarity vs. Strategy:** Is the team confused about the goal (Clarity), or is the goal fundamentally incoherent (Strategic Drift)? *Check $V$ before rewriting the team charter.*

## Application

In the [Table 2](../entities/table-2-diagnostics.md) diagnostic decision tree:
1. Identify the symptom.
2. Check the Macro Layer ($V, P, A$) first.
3. If Macro is clear, check the Micro Layer ($E$).
4. If ambiguous between Skill and Coordination inside $E$, fix Coordination first.
