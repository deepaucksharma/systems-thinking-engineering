# Lever-Access Rule (V2)

## Definition

Before pulling a lever prescribed by the [Fix-Order](../legacy/fix-order.md) hierarchy, the manager must verify they have the authority (access) to pull it. In **Canonical V2**, levers span all 5 Blocks ($V \cdot P \cdot E \cdot A \cdot L$). Engineering managers often only have direct access to $E$ (Execution) levers, while $V$ (Value) and $A$ (Org) levers are walled off by the executive layer. Attempting to pull a local lever when the root breakdown is a blocked Macro lever will cause systemic collapse.

## The Escalation Rule

If you cannot move a lever, particularly a **Macro-Gate** (Value, Org, People), you must **escalate as an explicit tradeoff** and name the tradeoff owner. 

Do not run an agile transformation locally (Block E) if the root failure is an [Incentive Death Spiral](incentive-death-spiral.md) (Block A). You must escalate the incentive boundary, or explicitly state: "We cannot optimize internal flow while the external dependency structure mandates friction."
