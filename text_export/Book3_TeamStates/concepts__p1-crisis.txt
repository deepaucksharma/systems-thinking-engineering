# P1 — Crisis

## Definition

The team is deep in the [Losing Loop](losing-loop.md). Characterized by confusion, overload, panic, and zero slack. 

## Symptoms

- No one can restate the goal clearly
- WIP is uncapped and growing
- Context switching is constant
- Quality bypasses are routine
- The team is reactive, not proactive

## Intervention (Execution Layer)

*Assuming the Crisis is strictly operational ($E$), deploy the Execution Playbook:*
1. **Stop adding work** — no new requests
2. **Choose ONE goal** — Direction lever, priority 5/5
3. **Cut WIP hard** — Protection lever, priority 5/5
4. **Ship one small win** — restore focus and flow

## Review Cadence
2×/week (per [Nyquist Constraint](../legacy/nyquist-constraint.md))

## Transitions
- → [P2-Stabilizing](p2-stabilizing.md): One priority holds ≥1 cycle, WIP under control
- → [P6-Mixed](p6-mixed-state.md): One team lane stabilizes while another is still burning

## V2 Multi-Dimensional Threshold

In Canonical V2, P1 specifically describes the state where the **Execution Order Parameter ($\Phi_E$)** drops below critical levels ($\Phi_E < 0.3$). 

**CRITICAL WARNING**: Before running the P1 Execution Intervention, you must verify the team isn't actually in a [V2 Extended State](v2-extended-states.md) structural collapse:
- If $\Phi_E$ is low but $\Phi_P \approx 0$ (Burnout/Fear), cracking down on WIP or Goals will only accelerate attrition. You must pull the Safety/Load lever.
- If the drop into P1 was caused by the [Incentive Death Spiral](incentive-death-spiral.md) ($\Phi_A$ collapse), team-level interventions will fail entirely.
