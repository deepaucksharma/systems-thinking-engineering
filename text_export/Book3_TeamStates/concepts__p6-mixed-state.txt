# P6 — Mixed State

## Definition

A genuinely distinct team configuration where **different subsystems are in different states simultaneously**. In V1, this meant one lane executing differently than another. In the V2 framework, P6 is massively expanded: sub-scores split across ranges within the multi-dimensional [Order Parameter ($\Phi$)](order-parameter.md). 

## V2 Extended Rule

**Original rule:** Use worst state for stabilization, best state for investment. Do NOT average away a zero.

**V2 extension:** Identify which Block is driving the worst score. The intervention must match the block. Never average sub-scores across blocks.
- Worst score in **P**: stabilize with psychological safety and load reduction.
- Worst score in **V**: stabilize with scope reduction and strategy clarification.
- Worst score in **A**: stabilize by documenting constraints and escalating; do not promise team-level fixes.

## Symptoms

- Averaged metrics say "functional" but one subsystem is burning.
- Cross-domain context switching.
- Heroics mask collapse.
- (V2) execution metrics are green ($\Phi_E$ high), but people metrics indicate burnout ($\Phi_P$ low). A team with $\Phi_P = 0.2$ and $\Phi_E = 0.9$ is not a "0.55 team."

## Mathematical Reality

Evaluating the overall Order Parameter ($\Phi$) by averaging metrics will lie. The manager must partition the metrics by lane/block constraint.
