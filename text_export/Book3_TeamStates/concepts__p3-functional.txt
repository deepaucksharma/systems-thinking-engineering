# P3 — Functional

## Definition

The default baseline for an operational team. Delivery mostly works. The team is neutral — neither spiraling in the [Losing Loop](losing-loop.md) nor accelerating in the [Winning Loop](winning-loop.md).

## Symptoms

- Delivery is predictable
- Quality is acceptable
- Ownership is clear
- No active crises, but limited improvement momentum

## Intervention

- Invest in Development (priority 4/5)
- Maintain Standards (priority 4/5)
- Protect improvement time

## Review Cadence
Biweekly

## Transitions

- → [P4-Thriving](p4-thriving.md): Sustained investment, improvement time protected
- → [P2-Stabilizing](p2-stabilizing.md): Slack filled with commitments, debt ignored
- → [P1-Crisis](p1-crisis.md): Stakeholder panic, surprise work injected

## V2 Multi-Dimensional Threshold

In Canonical V2, P3 represents a stable, laminar flow on the Execution vector ($0.5 \leq \Phi_E < 0.7$). 

However, in V2, a P3 execution state combined with $V \approx 0$ creates the **"Wrong Thing Fast"** [V2 Extended State](v2-extended-states.md). The team is functional, predictable, and calm, but mathematically outputting total waste. P3 is stable enough that leadership must begin focusing actively on Value ($V$) and Org Alignment ($A$) scaling.

It is also vulnerable to [Self-Organized Criticality](self-organized-criticality.md) where seemingly stable teams can collapse suddenly into Crisis if technical debt avalanches.
