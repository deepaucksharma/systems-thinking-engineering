# P5 — Scaling

## Definition

The [Winning Loop](winning-loop.md) is running but at risk. Growth is stressing existing structure — hiring, scope expansion, or reorg is creating friction that didn't exist before.

## Symptoms

- Structure starts creaking
- Onboarding bottlenecks appear
- Dependency explosion
- What worked at smaller scale no longer works

## Intervention

- Redesign Structure (priority 5/5)
- Re-clarify Direction (priority 5/5)
- Strengthen onboarding

## Transitions

- → [P4-Thriving](p4-thriving.md): New structure stable, onboarding repeatable, delivery predictable
- → [P2-Stabilizing](p2-stabilizing.md): Growth without ownership reset, dependency explosion

## Mathematical Threshold & Conway's Law

In Canonical V2, P5 occurs when the Execution vector is maximized ($\Phi_E \geq 0.85$) but its derivative is negative ($\frac{d\Phi_E}{dt} < 0$). 

This state is inextricably linked to **Org Alignment ($A$)**. The structure is under immense stress precisely because [Conway's Law](conways-law.md) constraints (sub-variables within Block A) are violated by team size scaling past architectural boundaries. 

Fixing P5 requires pulling the Structure lever and adjusting $A$, acknowledging that localized team execution ($E$) has hit the structural cap provided by the organization.
