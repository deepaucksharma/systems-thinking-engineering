# Cross-Domain Extensions

## Definition

While described using software terminology, the engineering framework's principles generalize mathematically to any complex knowledge work.

The common pattern exists in any domain where:
1. Work is **cognitively complex** (non-routine).
2. Quality failures have **delayed consequences** (driving a massive rework loop).
3. Coordination overhead scales nonlinearly with team size.
4. Output flow is stochastic due to high task variation.

## Mappings Across Domains

### Academic Research Labs
- **Clarity:** Research question alignment.
- **Focus:** Number of parallel research projects per post-doc.
- **Skill:** Domain expertise.
- **Coordination:** Cross-lab and grant collaborations.
- **Quality:** Peer review rigor.
- **Output:** Meaningful publications / fundamental discovery.

### Hospital Emergency Departments
- **Clarity:** Triage protocols.
- **Focus:** Patient load vs. capacity (direct application of [Little's Law](littles-law.md)).
- **Skill:** Medical and procedural training.
- **Coordination:** Handoff mapping (nurse to doctor to specialist).
- **Quality:** Treatment outcomes (error rate).
- **Output:** Stabilized patients delivered.

### Legal Firms
- **Clarity:** Case strategy definitions.
- **Focus:** Active caseload limits per attorney.
- **Skill:** Legal expertise context.
- **Coordination:** Partner to associate delegation tracks.
- **Quality:** Brief quality, precedent and evidentiary rigor.
- **Output:** Cases successfully won or resolved.
