# Plan: Deep Audit-Level Review of Wiki Content (Enhanced)

## Elevated Objectives
Conduct a **mathematical, empirical, and pragmatic deep audit** of the entire wiki that surpasses surface-level assessment. This review will:
- Validate every equation for dimensional consistency, mathematical rigor, and falsifiability
- Ground-check every claim against primary literature and empirical evidence
- Red-team framework assumptions for failure modes and edge cases
- Verify that every intervention proposed produces measurable, causal outcomes
- Map the entire dependency graph to detect silent contradictions
- Assess practitioner utility with concrete "can someone actually use this?" tests
- Score the framework on both theoretical soundness and real-world actionability

The output will be an audit report of publishable quality, not just a descriptive summary.

## Scope
Full exhaustive coverage of **all 82 files**. No sampling. No exceptions. Every sentence will be evaluated.
- **Core Framework**: Full mathematical validation of the 5 blocks, master equation, theorems, and metrics
- **Logical Consistency**: Cross-check every cross-reference, every causal claim, every gating relationship
- **Empirical Grounding**: Verify every claim against published literature or empirical evidence
- **Pragmatic Utility**: Assess every piece of guidance for implementability, measurability, and outcome predictability
- **Edge Case Coverage**: Explicitly test the framework against pathological cases, exceptions, and failure modes

## Enhanced Methodology
This is not a reading exercise. This is an audit:
1. **Formal Validation**: For every mathematical claim, check:
   - Dimensional consistency (units are compatible across multiplication)
   - Boundary conditions (what happens at 0, 1, infinity?)
   - Monotonicity (does increasing X always increase/decrease Y as claimed?)
   - Falsifiability (what evidence would prove this wrong?)
2. **Empirical Grounding**: For every assertion:
   - Is it citing established theory? (e.g., Nyquist, Little's Law, Edmondson)
   - Is there empirical evidence for the magnitude of effects?
   - Are there known counterexamples?
   - Are boundary conditions clearly stated?
3. **Red Teaming**: For every rule or principle:
   - When does this *not* apply?
   - What happens if you follow this advice incorrectly?
   - What are the second-order effects?
   - What is the worst way this could be misinterpreted?
4. **Coherence Testing**:
   - Map the entire directed acyclic graph of causal claims
   - Detect cycles, contradictions, and unreachable states
   - Verify that diagnostic flows actually terminate in actionable interventions
   - Ensure no claim in file A contradicts file B
5. **Pragmatic Assessment**:
   - Can a manager actually measure every variable proposed?
   - Are interventions specific enough to implement?
   - What is the expected feedback loop for every action?
   - What is the cost/benefit of each proposed intervention?
