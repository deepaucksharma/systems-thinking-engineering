# Canonical V2: Complete Consolidated Model

This source represents the comprehensive v2 framework for Engineering Management Control Theory. It establishes a multi-dimensional, multiplicative output equation that breaks system performance into five distinct blocks.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The V2 Master Equation**: $OUTCOME(t) = \theta \cdot V(t) \cdot P(t) \cdot E(t) \cdot A(t) \cdot L(t) \cdot e^{-\lambda \cdot Debt(t)} \cdot g(Var(t))$
2. **The Five Blocks**:
    - **Value Alignment (V)**: Are we building the right thing? Strategy and problem fit.
    - **People System (P)**: Can humans tell the truth and sustain? Safety, motivation, sustainability.
    - **Execution System (E)**: Can work flow? Clarity, focus, skill, coordination, quality. (This encompasses almost the entirety of the V1 "Master Equation").
    - **Org Alignment (A)**: Does the org permit this to work? Incentives, architecture.
    - **Learning (L)**: Do we improve or just repeat? Feedback speed, learning practices.
3. **Multiplicative Logic**: A zero in any block ("hard zero") collapses the entire system, rendering local optimizations elsewhere mathematically futile.
4. **New Gating Theorems**: Many structural measurements are "gated" by prerequisite underlying human states. Most prominently:
    - *Psychological Safety (Block P)* gates Learning (Block L). Without safety, data is falsified.
    - *Org Alignment (Block A)* gates Execution (Block E).
    - *Learning (Block L)* gates Value (Block V).
5. **State Dynamics**: The traditional P1-P6 state model is expanded since the "order parameter" ($\Phi$) is now multi-dimensional. Five new states describe different failure modes like "Wrong Thing Fast" (high E, low V) and "Sweatshop" (high E, low P).
6. **Nyquist Constraint Specifics**: The document formally applies the Nyquist constraint by listing specific time constants across system blocks—spanning from weeks for Focus (E) dynamics to years for Culture (P) and Org Structure (A).
