# V2 Theorems

This page collects the strongest claims made by the earlier V2 framework. They are best read as high-confidence operating propositions, not as formally proved mathematical theorems.

The name is retained for historical continuity, but the content should be interpreted with stricter epistemic hygiene than the title suggests.

For the cleaner current formulation, see [02 - Master Equation](02-master-equation.md), [20 - Diagnostic Sequence](20-diagnostic-sequence.md), and [06 - Time Constants](06-time-constants.md).

## Claim Types

Not all statements below are the same kind of claim:
- some are broad systems heuristics with strong practical backing;
- some are interpretations of ideas borrowed from mature fields;
- some are framework-internal synthesis claims that remain contestable.

Where a proposition depends heavily on the framework's own modeling choices, it should not be mistaken for an externally proved theorem.

## Core Propositions

1. **Multiplicative Constraint**  
   Output depends on multiple blocks interacting together. Severe weakness in one block can dominate performance even when the others look healthy.

2. **Measurement Reliability Depends on Safety**  
   When psychological safety is low, quantitative metrics become less trustworthy because people filter, game, or suppress reality. In those moments, interviews, observation, and other qualitative signals become more important.

3. **Sustainability Governs Decay**  
   When demand persistently exceeds recovery, current output is partly financed by future capacity. The system may appear stable while quietly degrading.

4. **Variability Can Matter More Than the Mean**  
   When delivery variability becomes extreme, improving the average is often less valuable than reducing the spread. Reliability is frequently the real bottleneck.

5. **Gating Relationships Matter**  
   Some capabilities only work when their prerequisites are intact:
   - learning depends on safety;
   - execution depends on structure;
   - strategy quality depends on learning in uncertain environments.

6. **Time Constants Differ by Layer**  
   Different parts of the system move at very different speeds. Team execution can shift faster than culture, strategy, or org structure. Many failed interventions are really failed expectations about timing.

7. **Escalation Is a Real Management Task**  
   When the root problem lives in strategy or structure, team-level coaching has bounded value. At that point, the manager's work is diagnosis, documentation, and escalation.

8. **Observation Windows Matter**  
   Re-intervening before a change has had time to express itself can turn the manager into the main source of instability. This is the practical meaning of the Nyquist-style warning in the framework.

9. **Conditions Drift Without Maintenance**  
   Healthy states do not usually persist on their own. Focus, clarity, trust, and quality all decay when neglected.

10. **Execution Does Not Rescue Bad Direction**  
    Strong local execution aimed at the wrong goal is still failure. Better throughput does not compensate for weak value alignment.

## How to Use This Page

Use these propositions as:
- a compact statement of the older V2 worldview;
- a way to compare legacy claims with the more careful current wording;
- a checklist for where managerial intuition often goes wrong.

Do not use this page as a substitute for the fuller current references. It is a summary of strong claims, not a complete operating manual.

## What This Page Does Not Establish

This page does not establish:
- universal numeric thresholds;
- formal proofs for the master-equation structure;
- unique causal direction between all blocks;
- a complete reduction of organizational behavior to one physical analogy.

It is a strong summary of an important model lineage. It is not a finished scientific theory.
