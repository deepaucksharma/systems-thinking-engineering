# P3 — Functional

## Definition

The default starting assumption for a team. Delivery mostly works. The team is neutral — neither in the [Losing Loop](losing-loop.md) nor fully in the [Winning Loop](winning-loop.md).

## Symptoms

- Delivery is predictable
- Quality is acceptable
- Ownership is clear
- No active crises, but limited improvement momentum

## Intervention

- Invest in Development (priority 4/5)
- Maintain Standards (priority 4/5)
- Protect improvement time

## Review Cadence

Biweekly

## Transitions

- → [P4-Thriving](p4-thriving.md): Sustained investment, improvement time protected, standards hold under pressure
- → [P2-Stabilizing](p2-stabilizing.md): Slack filled with commitments, debt ignored, loss of key experts
- → [P1-Crisis](p1-crisis.md): Stakeholder panic, surprise work injected
- → [P6-Mixed](p6-mixed-state.md): New domain added without redesigning ownership

## Mathematical Threshold
In the [Extended Framework](../sources/3-extended-framework.md), P3 exists in the $0.5 \leq \Phi < 0.7$ range of the [Order Parameter ($\Phi$)](order-parameter.md). It represents a stable, laminar flow. However, it is vulnerable to [Self-Organized Criticality](self-organized-criticality.md) where seemingly stable teams can collapse suddenly into Crisis if technical debt avalanches.
