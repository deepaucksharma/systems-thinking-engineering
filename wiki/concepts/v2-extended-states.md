# V2 Extended States

Because the V2 [Order Parameter ($\Phi$)](order-parameter.md) is multi-dimensional ($\Phi = [\Phi_V, \Phi_P, \Phi_E, \Phi_A, \Phi_L]$), system states are no longer purely linear scalar thresholds like P1-P5. The pattern of mismatched sub-scores determines specific, repeating organizational failure modes.

## The Five New Named States

| State | Pattern | Characteristics |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **Wrong Thing Fast** | $\Phi_V$ low, $\Phi_E$ high | Efficient waste production; will discover too late. The team operates perfectly but strategic fit is a zero. |
| **Sweatshop** | $\Phi_P$ low, $\Phi_E$ medium-high | Unsustainable performance; burning people for output. Overload loop is masking actual decay. |
| **Good Intentions** | $\Phi_V$ high, $\Phi_P$ high, $\Phi_E$ low | Great culture and strategy, bad execution. The team cannot actually deliver on its vision. |
| **Cynical Machine** | $\Phi_A$ low, $\Phi_E$ medium | Working hard inside a broken incentive system. Rational disengagement takes hold as people optimize for survival instead of value. |
| **Locally Optimal** | $\Phi_E$ high, $\Phi_V/\Phi_A$ medium | The team is great, but the org game above them is wrong. The team will eventually stop trying due to top-down impedance. |

## Intervention Strategy

These states are specific manifestations of the [P6 Mixed State](p6-mixed-state.md). A critical rule is to avoid intervening in the block that is working well. Trying to fix a "Sweatshop" by providing technical coaching ($\Phi_E$ intervention) completely ignores the underlying burnout ($\Phi_P$ collapse) and actually accelerates the crisis.
